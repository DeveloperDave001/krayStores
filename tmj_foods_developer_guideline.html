Developer Guideline

A complete specification for building a high-trust, easy-to-navigate eCommerce website for TMJ Foods, with Paystack payment integration for local and international customers.

Next.js 14 Paystack MongoDB Cloudinary Nigeria + Diaspora Mobile-First

Project Goals

Easy to Navigate

Any visitor — from a first-time buyer in Lagos to a diaspora customer in London — should find and buy in under 3 minutes.

Trust & Credibility

Visual design, security badges, testimonials, and transparent policies must make TMJ Foods feel completely safe to buy from.

Seamless Payments

Paystack handles all payment types: cards, bank transfer, USSD, and international cards — with zero friction at checkout.

Export-Ready

The shop must sell to customers across Nigeria and internationally — USD, GBP, and NGN pricing support.

Target Audiences

  • Health-conscious Nigerians — urban adults managing diabetes, weight, or gut health, mostly on mobile, primarily paying via bank transfer or card.
  • African diaspora — Nigerians abroad in UK, USA, Canada, Europe who want familiar, clean-label ingredients. Pay via international card.
  • Fitness & wellness community — gym-goers and athletes drawn to SORPLA AlphaGrain. Active on Instagram and TikTok.
  • Mothers & families — buying for children and elderly; need simple navigation, clear ingredients, and reassurance on quality.
  • Wholesale buyers & retailers — restaurant owners and health stores needing a bulk inquiry channel.

Recommended Tech Stack

Chosen for speed, developer familiarity, and solid Paystack ecosystem support.

Frontend

Next.js 14 (App Router)

React framework with built-in SSR, image optimisation, and SEO-friendly routing.

Flexible NoSQL for products, orders, and customers. Free tier handles launch-phase traffic.

Payments & Auth

Primary payment gateway. Handles NGN, USD, GBP. Cards, bank transfer, USSD, Apple Pay.

NextAuth.js

Optional customer accounts — email/password + Google OAuth. Guest checkout must always work.

Resend / Nodemailer

Transactional emails: order confirmations, shipping updates, receipts.

Hosting & DevOps

Zero-config Next.js deployment. Global CDN, automatic HTTPS, preview URLs for every PR.

GitHub Actions

CI/CD pipeline: lint → test → deploy to staging → production on merge to main.

Sentry

Error monitoring. Catch payment failures and broken pages before customers report them.

UX Principles

Every interaction decision should be guided by these principles.

The 3-Minute Rule

A first-time visitor must be able to arrive at the homepage, find a product, add it to cart, complete checkout, and receive a confirmation email — in under 3 minutes. Test this on a real mobile device at every major milestone.

Mobile-First Always

  • Design for 375px width first. Desktop is an enhancement, not the baseline.
  • Touch targets minimum 44px × 44px. "Add to Cart" button full-width on mobile.
  • No horizontal scroll anywhere on mobile. Test with Chrome DevTools + real devices.
  • Bottom navigation bar on mobile: [ Home ] [ Shop ] [ Cart ] [ Account ]

Key UX Rules

  • Cart as slide-out drawer — opening the cart should not navigate away from the current page. A right-side drawer overlays the page so the customer can keep browsing.
  • Guest checkout is mandatory — never force account creation. Offer it as an option after payment succeeds.
  • Progress indicator at checkout — show 3 steps: Delivery Info → Payment → Confirmation. Always show where the customer is.
  • Out-of-stock is never a dead end — show a "Notify me when available" email capture instead of a disabled button.
  • Empty states have CTAs — an empty cart says "Your cart is empty. Explore our products →" with a link to the Shop.
  • Loading states on every async action — adding to cart, submitting forms, processing payment all show a loading indicator. Never leave the user guessing.

Product Card Component

  • Show: product image (square, 1:1 ratio), product name, short description (max 2 lines), price in NGN (toggle to USD/GBP), star rating, and an "Add to Cart" button.
  • On hover (desktop): a subtle lift shadow and a quick-view icon. On touch (mobile): immediate tap response with visual feedback.
  • Display health benefit tags under the product name — e.g. Gluten-FreeLow GIGut Health
  • Out-of-stock products show a grey overlay with "Notify Me" — never remove them from the grid.

Product Detail Page Must Have

  • Image gallery — main image with thumbnail strip. Support swipe on mobile. Include lifestyle/usage photos alongside the pack shot.
  • Functional benefits section — visually distinct icons or pills for each health benefit. This is what TMJ Foods is selling — make it prominent.
  • How to use — a brief cooking/serving guide. Customers often don't know how to use plantain flour.
  • Ingredients & nutrition facts — in an expandable accordion section. Required for food products.
  • Customer reviews — star rating breakdown + individual review cards. Show average prominently near the Add to Cart button.
  • Related products — "Customers also bought" carousel of 4 products at the bottom.
  • Quantity selector — allow 1–12 units. Default to 1.

Cart & Checkout

  • Show a "Free delivery on orders over ₦15,000" banner inside the cart with a progress bar showing how close they are.

Form Rules

  • Inline validation — validate each field on blur, not on submit. Show errors immediately in red below the field.
  • Nigerian phone numbers — accept formats: 08012345678, +2348012345678, 2348012345678. Auto-normalise before saving.

  • Order summary always visible — on desktop: sticky right column. On mobile: collapsible summary at top of checkout page.

Server-Side Initialization

pages/api/checkout.js
// POST /api/checkout
// Called when customer clicks "Pay Now"
export default async function handler(req, res) {
  const { cartItems, customerEmail, deliveryAddress } = req.body;
  
  // 1. Calculate total on the server (never trust client-side total)
  const totalKobo = calculateTotal(cartItems) * 100; // Paystack uses kobo
  
  // 2. Create order in DB with status: "pending"
  const order = await Order.create({
    items: cartItems,
    total: totalKobo / 100,
    customer: { email: customerEmail, address: deliveryAddress },
    status: "pending",
    reference: generateRef() // e.g. "TMJ-1712345678-ABCD"
  });
  
  // 3. Initialize Paystack transaction
  const response = await fetch("https://api.paystack.co/transaction/initialize", {
    method: "POST",
    headers: {
      Authorization: `Bearer ${process.env.PAYSTACK_SECRET_KEY}`,
      "Content-Type": "application/json",
    },
    body: JSON.stringify({
      email: customerEmail,
      amount: totalKobo,
      reference: order.reference,
      currency: "NGN", // or "USD" / "GBP" for international
      callback_url: `${process.env.BASE_URL}/order/success`,
      metadata: { orderId: order._id.toString() }
    }),
  });
  
  const data = await response.json();
  return res.json({ authorizationUrl: data.data.authorization_url, reference: order.reference });
}

Webhook Handler

pages/api/webhooks/paystack.js
import crypto from "crypto";

export default async function handler(req, res) {
  // 1. Verify webhook signature (CRITICAL — prevents fraud)
  const hash = crypto
    .createHmac("sha512", process.env.PAYSTACK_SECRET_KEY)
    .update(JSON.stringify(req.body))
    .digest("hex");

  if (hash !== req.headers["x-paystack-signature"]) {
    return res.status(401).json({ error: "Invalid signature" });
  }

  const { event, data } = req.body;

  if (event === "charge.success") {
    // 2. Verify the transaction independently (never trust webhook alone)
    const verify = await fetch(`https://api.paystack.co/transaction/verify/${data.reference}`, {
      headers: { Authorization: `Bearer ${process.env.PAYSTACK_SECRET_KEY}` }
    });
    const { data: txn } = await verify.json();

    if (txn.status === "success") {
      // 3. Update order status to "paid"
      await Order.findOneAndUpdate(
        { reference: data.reference },
        { status: "paid", paidAt: new Date(), paystackId: txn.id }
      );
      // 4. Send confirmation email
      await sendOrderConfirmationEmail(txn.metadata.orderId);
      // 5. Reduce stock counts
      await updateInventory(txn.metadata.orderId);
    }
  }

  return res.status(200).json({ received: true });
}

Security Rules (Non-Negotiable)

  • CRITICAL Always verify the Paystack webhook signature using HMAC SHA-512. Reject any webhook that fails.
  • CRITICAL Always independently verify transactions on the server using the Paystack Verify endpoint. Never fulfil an order based on client-side callback alone.
  • CRITICAL Store PAYSTACK_SECRET_KEY in environment variables only. Never commit it to source code or expose it to the frontend.
  • IMPORTANT Calculate order totals on the server from the database price, not from values sent by the frontend. A user could manipulate the frontend price.
  • IMPORTANT Use idempotency — check if an order is already marked "paid" before processing a webhook, to prevent double-fulfilment from duplicate webhook deliveries.
  • IMPORTANT Use Paystack's Test Keys during development. Only switch to Live Keys in the production environment.

Checkout Trust Reinforcement

At the point of payment — the highest-anxiety moment for any online shopper — these elements must be visible:

  • Order summary with item names, quantities, and prices on the right side of the checkout page.
  • "Your payment is secured by Paystack" with the Paystack logo directly above the Pay Now button.
  • Accepted payment method icons: Visa, Mastercard, Verve, bank transfer, USSD.
  • A brief reassurance line: "We will never store your card details. All payments are processed securely by Paystack."
  • Customer support phone number or WhatsApp link visible in case of any issue during checkout.
  • Clear total breakdown: Subtotal + Delivery Fee + Discount (if any) = Grand Total. No surprise charges.

Error Handling for Failed Payments

  • If Paystack returns a failed payment: show a clear, friendly error message — "Your payment didn't go through. Please try again or choose a different payment method."
  • Never say "Error 402" or technical jargon. Plain English always.
  • Offer the customer to: try again with same method, choose a different payment method, or contact support via WhatsApp.
  • The cart and delivery details must be preserved — never make the customer re-enter their information after a failed payment.

SEO & Performance

A fast, discoverable site is part of building trust. These are the baseline requirements.

Core Web Vitals Targets

LCP (Largest Contentful Paint)

Target: under 2.5 seconds. Optimise hero image loading — use Next.js priority prop on homepage hero.

CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift)

Target: under 0.1. Set explicit width/height on all images. No layout shifts during font loading.

FID (First Input Delay)

Target: under 100ms. Minimise JavaScript bundle size. Use code splitting and lazy loading.

SEO Checklist

  • Meta titles and descriptions — unique for every page. Product pages: "[Product Name] — Buy Online | TMJ Foods Nigeria". Max 60 chars for title, 160 for description.
  • Open Graph tags — required for all pages. When shared on WhatsApp or social media, every page should show a proper preview image, title, and description.
  • Structured data (JSON-LD) — Product schema on product pages (name, price, availability, rating). BreadcrumbList on inner pages. Organization schema on homepage.
  • Canonical URLs — prevent duplicate content. Set canonical tags on all paginated or filtered shop views.
  • XML Sitemap — auto-generated at /sitemap.xml. Include all products, blog posts, and main pages. Submit to Google Search Console on launch.
  • robots.txt — block /api/, /admin/, and /checkout/ from indexing. Allow everything else.
  • Image alt text — every product image must have a descriptive alt attribute. E.g. "TMJ Foods Sorghum-Plantain Flour 1kg pack".
  • Blog content — write posts targeting search terms like "unripe plantain flour health benefits", "gluten-free Nigerian food", "sorghum flour for diabetics Nigeria".
